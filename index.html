About Me

I am a teaching professor at the University of Cincinnati where I'm taking on technical education for PhD students in the Psychology Department and looking for other opportunities to mix my love for human behavior with the development and implementation of spatial computing technologies.

I was a post-doctoral researcher with Jon Matthis in the Human Movement Neuroscience Lab at Northeastern University from Fall 2020-Spring 2023. I received my PhD in Cognitive Science from Brown University in the Winter of 2021.

During my post-doc, I Co-Founded The FreeMoCap Project - creating free and open source motion capture for everyone. I'm the Chief Operations Officer of our Non-Profit, The FreeMoCap Foundation, where I lead in project management and champion usability.

In my free time, I enjoy playing D&D with my friends, going on hikes with my family, and playing ball with my dog, Tasha.
